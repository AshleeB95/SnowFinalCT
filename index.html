Is climate change to blame for lack of snow over time?

The environment is ever changing and lack of snow could leave a drastic impact.

By: Ashlee Brown

Dec. 19, 2022
Aerial shot of snow covered trees and an empty field. | Source: Pexels

In the 2022-2023 winter season, there's going to be less snow in Connecticut, according to a News 12 meteorologist. A reason for the decline in total snowfall is a result of climate change. The Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) states more winter precipitation is falling in the form of rain instead of snow nationwide. Forty-eight states have reported experiencing a decrease in the proportion of precipitation falling as snow.

News 12 Meteorologist Jonathan Cubit says drastic changes to the environment including global warming are partially to blame for lack of snow over the years. "With warmer winters, a lot of early season storms will result in more rain events instead of snow. This doesn't mean we won't see a big winter storm producing 6-12" every now and then, but the frequency has been less over the years," says Cubit.

Applied Climate Information System (ACIS) data from 2021 shows total snow decreased by 55% since 2011.

Weather in Connecticut for the 2022-2023 winter season has been forecasted to be at or around average precipitation wise, according to Cubit. He says the weather is also "at or above average temperatures," which means there's a better chance of "at or below average snowfall totals."
